Badges render inside the right-aligned equation (tag), e.g. \veq{edot}\lean → (edot ✓). The glyph is shape-distinct (? / ∘ / △ / ✓ / ✓✓) and is the primary channel — so WCAG 1.4.1 (never colour alone) holds under every palette below; colour is reinforcement. The five open-debt \<tier>c variants (\sympyc …, "verifier desired, not yet run") reuse the same hue as their discharged tier and are told apart by the existing ᵖ-style superscript ? glyph — not a separate colour — so each palette stays five colours.

Accessibility summary (measured, not asserted)

Contrast is WCAG 2.1 against the minima light background #fdfdfd. "min deut" = the smallest pairwise separation among the five badges under simulated deuteranopia (Machado 2009, severity 1.0) — a low number means two badges look alike to a red-green-colour-blind reader and the reader must fall back on the glyph.

Honest finding: five saturated categorical hues dark enough to clear 4.5:1 on a white page cannot all stay separated under dichromacy — that is why B (categorical, colour-blind-source) scores worse on min-separation than C (an ordinal luminance ramp). If colour-blind readability is the deciding factor, C is the recommendation; if intuitive semantics matter most, A. B is offered as the familiar 5-distinct-hue middle. In all three the glyph carries the meaning regardless.
